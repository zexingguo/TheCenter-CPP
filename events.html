Cyber Security and Awareness Fair

Location: Cal Poly Pomona
Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/cppcsf/
Wesbite: http://www.cpp.edu/~cyberfair/

The Cyber Security and Awareness Fair is held annually at Cal Poly Pomona in order to bring awareness to the world of Cyber Security where our entire world is progressing into an internet world. The event is open to all ages, especially high school to university-level students. The fair brings in speakers from Cyber Security fields, holds symposiums, and has hands-on exhibits.

HackPoly

Location: Bronco Student Center, Cal Poly Pomona
Sponsored: Cal Poly Founders
Date: February 11-12, 2017

Hackpoly is a 24-hour annual hackathon event held at Cal Poly Pomona. Participants create teams to create a project, software or hardware, in an environment optimized for creativity. Teams then demonstrate their projects to fellow hackers, visitors, and a panel of judges to compete for first, second, and third place. Find out more...

National Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition

Wesbite: http://www.nationalccdc.org/index.php
Presented : Raytheon Find Out More..
Date: 2017

The top teams from each Regional CCDC (Western, Pacific Rim, North Central, Rocky Mountain, Southwest, Mid-West, Southeast, Mid-Atlantic, Northeast) compete for first place at the National CCDC.

Western Regional Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition

Wesbite: www.wrccdc.org
Sponsors: Facebook Find Out More..
Date: March 24-26, 2017

The competition brings college teams from all over the western region where the top 8 qualifying teams experience two days of maintaining systems, management demands, customer and client support, and defending against attacks.

GenCyber HS

Location: Cal Poly Pomona
Contact: cppgencyber@gmail.com
Wesbite: https://www.cpp.edu/~sfs/gencyber.shtml

To expand the knowledge of students K-12 about the concepts of cybersecurity and forensics, the Cal Poly Pomona SFS team plans to organize a GenCyber camp for secondary students and teachers to introduce fundamentals of operating systems and networking concepts and show the application of such topics through hands-on activities. The camp will also include speakers on cybersecurity competitions, certifications, careers, and curriculum development. These activities are supported from the National Security Agency and National Science Foundation. Find out more...
